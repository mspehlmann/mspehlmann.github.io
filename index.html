About Me

I am an exploration geologist with 10 years of experience across Alaska. This has provided me with a deep understanding of greenstone gold, VMS, skarn, and most recently, ultramafic layered intrusion deposit models and how they manifest in Alaska. Recently, I decided to return to school to pursue my interest in geostatistics, particularly as applied to domain modeling and resource estimation. I am currently a second-year graduate student in statistics at the University of Alaska Fairbanks. My research interests include exploring the applicability of process convolutions for generating resource estimations and identifying exploration targets.

Services

I am an independent geological consultant specializing in project management across Alaska. My decade of experience as a geologist has made me a versatile asset, whether for early-stage exploration projects or active production sites. I am skilled in managing remote exploration programs and production geology at operating mines. I excel at developing greenfield exploration programs, from geological mapping to geophysics, as well as conducting first-pass drill programs. As my career advances, I am increasingly focused on applying my statistical expertise to exploration challenges. I am particularly interested in developing and applying statistical or geostatistical tools to aid in target generation.
