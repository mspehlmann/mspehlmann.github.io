KoBold

I was an operations lead for KoBold's Skolai Project in the Alaska Range. In this role, I oversaw all field and camp activities for a geological reconnaissance program and a ground EM survey. This was my first experience working with an ultramafic layered intrusion deposit model. I made the most of my free time to learn about this new deposit type by accompanying the field geologists whenever possible.

Constantine

I was the project geologist for the Palmer Project during the 2023 infill drill program. This is a VMS deposit located in the Wrangellia Terrane of Southeast Alaska. My primary responsibilities included ensuring the drill program ran efficiently, overseeing core logging, and ensuring samples were dispatched regularly.

Kinross

I was the project geologist for Kinross's greenfield exploration of the Tetlin Reservation, leading a geochemical stream and soil survey in rugged terrain near the Denali Fault. During the winter, I transitioned to overseeing the infill drilling program for what is now the Manh Choh mine. This was my first experience conducting a surface drill program in the harsh conditions of interior Alaska.

Coeur

Shortly after finishing my undergraduate degree, I took a contract position in Juneau with Coeur Alaska in the fall of 2014. I assisted the database manager with QA/QC of historic assays and maps. When the contract ended in the winter, I continued as a geotech to build my geology skills, often working in the roles of ore control geologist and core logger. From 2015 to 2017, I progressed into a full-time role as a mine geologist.

As a mine geologist, I was responsible for daily ore control in a narrow vein underground setting, planning infill and stope fence drilling, and making stope resource estimates. During this period, I developed a keen interest in implicit domaining and geostatistics. In 2019, I presented my structural model of the controlling faults at Kensington at the Alaska Miner's Association convention.

At the end of 2020, I was promoted to senior exploration geologist. I led an effort to review the geochemistry of the diorite pluton, identifying a unique phase change previously undocumented. Given that the Berners Bay district has been intermittently mined for over a century, I investigated soil geochemistry data contaminated by tram lines. Using multivariate statistical anomaly detection, I identified genuine geological anomalies, leading to a drill target that intercepted a vein.
